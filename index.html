Why sodium-ion over lithium-ion

Na-ion (CATL Naxtra) LFP

Energy density isn't the whole story.

LFP still leads on pack energy density — and we show it. But for large-scale, long-duration, cold-climate storage, the axes that decide lifetime economics and safety favour sodium: cycle life, low-temperature performance, and a sovereign, lithium-free supply chain.

Thermal safety is close — both chemistries pass no-fire penetration testing — so we don't overstate it.

Mature vs emerging. LFP's cathode was reported by Goodenough's group in 1997 — a ~30-year-mature chemistry whose 170 mAh/g theoretical capacity leaves it near its practical ceiling (~185 Wh/kg), which is why the field now reaches for manganese-doped LMFP for marginal gains. Sodium-ion reached mass production only in 2025 (CATL Naxtra) and retains materials headroom. The science is comparably old — Na and Li were both studied from the 1970s–80s — so sodium is newer to commercial maturity, not to research.
