§ 01  —  Problem

You're trusting three parties to be honest about the same battery.

Optimizer

Reports its own revenue

Revenue is easy to attribute. Damage is not. The optimizer chooses how hard to cycle your asset — and reports on its own performance. Conflict of interest, measured by the same party being measured.

OEM / BMS

Controls the degradation data

State-of-health numbers come from the same vendor who owes you a capacity guarantee. When disputes arise, the evidence lives on their side of the fence.

You

Sign 15-year contracts on their word

LTSAs, warranties, augmentation schedules, financing covenants — all priced on assumptions that nobody on your side can independently verify. Until now.

§ 02  —  What it does

Six capabilities, one source of truth for the asset.

01

Optimizer benchmarking

Revenue-adjusted-for-degradation
Compare optimizer strategies on the metric that actually matters: revenue per cycle-equivalent and per percentage-point of SoH lost. Detect when an optimizer is pushing your asset harder than contracted to inflate its own numbers. Evaluate bids from competing optimizers on like-for-like data before you sign.
02

Battery performance evaluation

OEM-agnostic, cell-to-pack
Rank cell suppliers and integrators on real field data across your portfolio: realized throughput, calendar vs. cyclic aging split, thermal behaviour, warranty margin consumption. Feed that evidence into procurement for the next project.
03

LTSA renegotiation data

Long-term service agreements
Service fees, capacity targets, availability definitions, and response-time clauses — priced on assumptions that age badly once the asset is in the ground. We quantify those assumptions against reality and hand you the leverage to reopen terms.
04

Augmentation decision support

When, how much, at what cost
Forward-projected SoH per string and per pack, with confidence intervals. Quantify the revenue uplift of augmenting in year N vs. N+1, against capex and contractual triggers, and stress-test against market price scenarios.
05

Warranty enforcement & contractual evidence

Defensible, timestamped, exportable
When delivered revenue underperforms or cells age faster than the warranty curve, you need a clean evidentiary record — not a screenshot from a vendor dashboard. We produce documentation that holds up in contractual disputes and, if it comes to it, legal proceedings.
06

Financing & risk reporting

Lender-ready, periodic
Independent performance reports for lenders and equity partners. We believe a credible, third-party intelligence layer on the asset is a lever on financing terms — early conversations with financiers support this, and we are actively validating it. (Status: thesis under validation.)
