The problem

Every battery ages differently in time,
and yet every trade prices it the same.

An accurate wear cost of a BESS requires a deep physical and electrochemical understanding of underlying degradation mechanisms coupled with environmental dependencies and computation fast enough for trading cadence. Most desks fall back to static, Li-ion-trained heuristics — wrong chemistry, wrong price.

EVERY DISPATCH DECISION — € for the kWh sold now  vs  € of battery life consumed. Sharper wear cost → better trades → lower LCOS.
BESS owner

Maximises lifetime profit

Horizon: 10–20 years. However a profitable trade and unprofitable trade look identical. Unpriced, every cycle's true cost stays invisible.

Trader

Maximises contracted time

Horizon: the contract. With a fixed wear proxy, the rational bid erodes the asset it trades on.

30–50% of lifetime profit forfeited by a mismatched static wear price — even with a perfect ageing model (Kumtepeli et al., 2024)

Category forming. Axpo and Entrix now offer BESS asset optimisation, but this creates a conflict of interest — traders optimise for revenue, often at the expense of long-term asset health.

Analytics and trading firms are increasingly partnering — for example, volytica and enspired (January 2025) — yet they still typically provide only static wear costs or fixed operating envelopes.

Independent providers such as ACCURE and TWAICE offer unbiased wear assessment and trading insights. However, they require deep SCADA integration, and their effectiveness is limited by restricted signals from standard BMS systems and incomplete cell degradation data.

As a BESS manufacturer with our own full technology stack — including advanced BMS, careful cell selection, and deep proprietary degradation modelling — we have superior real-time visibility and control. We succeed when our batteries deliver strong revenue and last longer.

No one provides a live, open, forward-looking degradation price. We close that gap.

Software — battery intelligence layer

Built on deep expertise in battery electrochemistry, advanced control algorithms, and machine learning

Our software combines two complementary capabilities. First, it continuously analyses battery operational data to monitor state of health, estimate degradation, detect anomalies using machine learning, and provide actionable insights into battery performance. Second, it translates this technical understanding into a real-time economic valuation by calculating a forward-looking marginal wear cost and dynamic operating envelope. Through a cloud API, this information can be integrated directly into trading and optimisation platforms, enabling bids based on the true cost of battery degradation rather than static Li-ion assumptions or warranty-derived defaults. Powered by our proprietary Na-ion electrochemical ageing model—an extension of the Oxford-developed SLIDE/PyBaMM framework calibrated with our own laboratory and field data—the platform helps maximise both battery lifetime and lifetime asset value.

Why Na-ion

Why sodium-ion over lithium-ion.

Na-ion LFP

The best battery depends on the application.

For stationary energy storage, sodium-ion chemistry offers compelling advantages, including excellent low-temperature performance, long cycle life, and the use of abundant, lithium-free materials. From a safety perspective, both sodium-ion and LFP can achieve high standards, including no-fire penetration performance, making long-term performance and lifetime economics key differentiators.
